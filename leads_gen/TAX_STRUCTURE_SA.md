## How SA Tax Works: Company vs Personal

### 1. Company Tax (Corporate Tax)
- **Rate:** 27% (for companies with taxable income > R91,250)
- **Small business:** 0-7% for companies with taxable income < R91,250
- **Applied to:** Company profit (revenue - expenses)

### 2. Personal Income Tax
- **Rate:** Progressive (18% - 45% depending on income)
- **Applied to:** Salary, dividends, or other personal income

## Tax Scenario 1: Pay Yourself a Salary

### Company Side:
- **Profit before salary:** R5,422,800
- **Your salary:** R600,000/year (R50,000/month)
- **Company profit after salary:** R5,422,800 - R600,000 = **R4,822,800**
- **Company tax (27%):** R4,822,800 × 27% = **R1,302,156**
- **Company profit after tax:** R4,822,800 - R1,302,156 = **R3,520,644**

### Personal Side:
- **Salary:** R600,000/year
- **Personal income tax:** ~R150,000 (varies by deductions)
- **Net take-home:** ~R450,000/year

### Total Tax Paid:
- Company tax: R1,302,156
- Personal tax: R150,000
- **Total:** R1,452,156

### Net After All Taxes:
- Company retained: R3,520,644
- Personal take-home: R450,000
- **Total:** R3,970,644

## Tax Scenario 2: Pay Yourself Dividends (More Tax-Efficient)

### Company Side:
- **Profit:** R5,422,800
- **Company tax (27%):** R5,422,800 × 27% = **R1,464,156**

- **Dividend paid:** R600,000
- **Dividend tax:** R600,000 × 20% = **R120,000** (withheld by company)

- Company tax: R1,464,156
- Dividend tax: R120,000
- **Total:** R1,584,156

### Net After All Taxes:
- Company retained: R3,358,644 (R3,958,644 - R600,000 dividend)
- Personal take-home: R480,000
- **Total:** R3,838,644

**Note:** Dividends are slightly less tax-efficient than salary in this example, but offer more flexibility.

## Key Tax Considerations

### 1. Company Structure
- **Pty Ltd:** Standard structure, 27% corporate tax
- **Sole Proprietor:** Personal tax rates (not recommended for high income)
- **Trust:** Can be tax-efficient but complex

### 2. Deductible Expenses
**Can deduct from company revenue:**
- ✅ VAPI costs (R1.2M/year)
- ✅ Team salaries (R3M/year)
- ✅ Office rent
- ✅ Software subscriptions
- ✅ Marketing costs
- ✅ Professional fees (accountant, lawyer)
- ✅ Equipment (computers, phones)
- ✅ Travel (business-related)
- ✅ Training/education

**Cannot deduct:**
- ❌ Personal expenses
- ❌ Fines/penalties
- ❌ Entertainment (limited)

### 3. Small Business Tax Relief
- **First R91,250:** 0% tax
- **R91,251 - R365,000:** 7% tax
- **R365,001+:** 27% tax

**If your company makes < R365K profit, you pay less tax!**

## Tax-Efficient Strategies

### 1. Maximize Deductions
- **Equipment:** Buy computers, phones, office furniture (deductible)
- **Training:** Business courses, conferences (deductible)
- **Home office:** If you work from home, claim portion of rent/utilities
- **Vehicle:** If used for business, claim portion of costs

### 2. Salary vs Dividends
- **Salary:** Deductible for company, taxable for you (progressive rates)
- **Dividends:** Not deductible for company, 20% flat tax for you
- **Optimal:** Mix of both (modest salary + dividends)

### 3. Retirement Contributions
- **Company contributions:** Deductible for company
- **Personal contributions:** Tax-deductible up to 27.5% of income
- **Benefit:** Reduces taxable income

### 4. Timing
- **Defer income:** If possible, delay invoicing to next tax year
- **Accelerate expenses:** Buy equipment before year-end
- **Tax year:** March 1 - February 28 (SA tax year)

## Important Notes

### 1. VAT (Value Added Tax)
- **Threshold:** Must register if revenue > R1M/year
- **Rate:** 15%
- **Impact:** You charge VAT on sales, claim VAT on purchases
- **Net effect:** Usually neutral (you pass VAT to customers)

### 2. PAYE (Pay As You Earn)
- **Applied to:** Salaries
- **Rate:** Withheld by company, remitted to SARS
- **Your responsibility:** Ensure PAYE is deducted and paid

### 3. Dividends Tax
- **Rate:** 20%
- **Withheld by:** Company (paid to SARS)
- **No personal return needed:** Already taxed at source

### 4. Provisional Tax
- **When:** If company tax > R1,000
- **Payments:** Twice yearly (August & February)
- **Based on:** Estimated annual tax liability
